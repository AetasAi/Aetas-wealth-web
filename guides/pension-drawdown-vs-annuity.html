Pension drawdown vs annuity options explained

A plain-English comparison of the main retirement income options.

Short answer: Drawdown keeps your pension invested and gives you flexible income. An annuity converts your pension into a guaranteed income for life. Both have advantages and both carry risks. The right approach depends on your income needs, attitude to risk, health, estate position and — now — the Finance Act 2026 inheritance tax changes.

Flexi-access drawdown

Flexi-access drawdown keeps your pension fund invested. You take income directly from the fund as needed — withdrawing more in years of higher expenditure and less in quieter years. The fund remains invested throughout, with the potential for continued growth. Any unspent fund passes to your chosen beneficiaries on death.

Drawdown is flexible. You control the timing and amount of withdrawals. But the fund is subject to investment risk: if markets fall, the value of your pension pot falls with them. Poor performance, combined with high withdrawals, can deplete a drawdown fund faster than anticipated — a risk known as sequencing risk.

Drawdown is appropriate for clients who are comfortable with investment risk, whose income needs are flexible, and who value the ability to pass unused pension wealth to the next generation. It requires ongoing management and regular review.

Lifetime annuity

A lifetime annuity converts your pension pot — or a portion of it — into a guaranteed income for the rest of your life. Once purchased, the income is fixed (or inflation-linked, if that option is selected). It cannot be reversed.

Annuities provide certainty. You will receive a known income regardless of how long you live or how markets perform. This can be valuable for clients who want a reliable income floor, or who are concerned about living longer than expected.

The trade-off is inflexibility. Once an annuity is purchased, the capital is gone — it cannot be accessed in a lump sum and it will not pass to beneficiaries in the same way that a drawdown fund can (unless enhanced death benefit options are selected, which reduce the income rate). Annuity rates reflect prevailing interest rates and your health at the time of purchase.

Flexible Pension Annuity

A Flexible Pension Annuity is a more recent product structure that seeks to combine elements of both approaches. It provides a guaranteed minimum income — addressing the certainty concern — while retaining some participation in investment performance and some degree of flexibility. It is designed for clients who want a guaranteed floor but are not ready to commit entirely to a fixed annuity.

The Finance Act 2026 dimension

From 6 April 2027, unspent pension funds — including funds held in flexi-access drawdown — will be brought within the scope of inheritance tax for the first time. This is a fundamental change to the way pension wealth interacts with estate planning.

Previously, keeping wealth in a pension drawdown arrangement was a tax-efficient estate planning strategy: the pension sat outside the estate and passed to beneficiaries free of IHT. After April 2027, the same fund will potentially be subject to inheritance tax at 40%. This changes the drawdown vs annuity decision for many clients, and the decision about how much to draw each year and how to sequence income from different sources.

Anyone with a material pension pot in drawdown, or approaching the point of choosing between drawdown and an annuity, should take professional advice before April 2027.

Key considerations

  • Income certainty — if you need a reliable income floor regardless of markets or longevity, an annuity provides that; drawdown does not.
  • Flexibility — if your expenditure is likely to vary significantly, or you want the ability to take ad hoc lump sums, drawdown is more appropriate.
  • Longevity risk — annuities guarantee income for life; drawdown funds can run out if withdrawals are too high or returns too low.
  • Investment risk — drawdown funds remain exposed to market performance; annuities eliminate this at the cost of potential upside.
  • Estate planning — post-April 2027, both drawdown and annuity have different IHT implications; a review of the estate position is important.
  • Health — enhanced annuity rates are available for clients with certain health conditions; this can significantly improve the annuity income available.

When professional advice may help

The drawdown vs annuity decision is one of the most significant financial choices a person makes. Getting it wrong — or making it without understanding all the implications — can have consequences that are difficult or impossible to reverse. With the Finance Act 2026 changes adding a new dimension, professional advice is particularly important for anyone approaching retirement in the next two to three years.
