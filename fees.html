How we charge

We believe you should know what our advice costs before you commit to it. This page explains how we charge for an initial review, for the work that follows, and for the ongoing service we provide afterwards.

At a glance

Three things to know about our fees

The detail follows below, but the headlines are simple.

  • Your first conversation is free. No charge, no obligation, and no recommendations made.
  • If we go further, the initial review fee is £995, refundable. If you decide to act on our recommendations, we waive this charge in part or in full.
  • Ongoing service is 1% a year of investments under our care, with a minimum and a cap that depends on the service level you choose.

We accept fees by card, bank transfer or cheque. Most clients prefer to pay through the platform that holds their investments, which we can arrange. We do not accept cash.

Stage 1

Initial consultation — free

Your first meeting with us is free. There is no obligation, no recommendations made and no products discussed. The aim is to understand your situation and decide together whether we are the right fit.

In the conversation we will:

  • Ask about your current financial arrangements
  • Understand your goals and what you want to achieve
  • Discuss your investment knowledge and how you feel about risk
  • Confirm how we could support you, and what the next steps would be

What it costs

£0

No charge for the first conversation, whether or not it leads anywhere.

Stage 2

Full financial review and recommendation

If you decide to take things further, we prepare a detailed review of your finances and a written set of recommendations tailored to your circumstances.

This includes:

  • Reviewing your existing arrangements
  • Modelling your investment strategy against your goals and your risk profile
  • Assessing whether existing holdings are still suitable
  • A written report explaining everything we recommend and why
  • A meeting to walk you through it in detail

What it costs

Notional cost

£995

The review is several hours of work — gathering information, modelling scenarios and writing the recommendation. We list this at £995 because it has a real cost to us.

In practice, most clients pay nothing. If you decide to act on our recommendations, this fee is waived in part or in full against the implementation charge.

If you are already a client, the fee never applies.

Protection Planning

How we charge for protection advice

Protection products such as life cover, critical illness and income protection are usually paid for through commission from the insurer rather than a fee from you. We will always tell you the amount of commission we will receive before any policy is put in place.

You do not pay anything upfront for protection advice, but the cost of the commission is built into the premiums you pay. Where this applies, we explain it in your suitability report along with the maximum we could reclaim from you and the timescale in which we could do so.

VAT. Our advice fees are usually exempt from VAT. Where VAT applies, we will tell you in advance and include it in any quote.

Important. The Financial Conduct Authority does not regulate Wills, Trusts or Tax advice. The value of your investments can go down as well as up, so you could get back less than you invested. The figures above are illustrative. Your own fees will be confirmed in writing in our client agreement and suitability report before any work is undertaken.
