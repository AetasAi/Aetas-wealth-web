Finance Act 2026: what the inheritance tax changes mean for you

A practical guide to the inheritance tax changes introduced in the Finance Act 2026, and what they mean for your estate planning.

10 May 2026 · 6 minute read

[Replace with article introduction. The Finance Act 2026 introduced several significant changes to the way inheritance tax applies in the UK. For many families, the practical impact is meaningful, and worth thinking about now rather than later.]

What's changed

[Replace with article content. The headline changes are: frozen nil-rate bands extended through to 2030, capped business and agricultural property relief, and the inclusion of pension assets in estates from April 2027.]

1. Frozen nil-rate bands

[Replace with article content. The £325,000 nil-rate band and £175,000 residence nil-rate band, frozen since 2009 and 2020 respectively, will remain frozen until 2030. As estates grow, more families will move into the IHT net.]

2. Capped business and agricultural property relief

[Replace with article content. From April 2026, BPR and APR have been capped at £1m of qualifying assets per individual, with relief above that level reduced to 50%.]

3. Pensions included from April 2027

[Replace with article content. From April 2027, defined contribution pension savings will form part of the deceased's estate for IHT purposes. This is a significant shift from current rules and changes how pensions sit within estate plans.]
