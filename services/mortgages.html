Mortgages

Mortgages are not just about the lowest rate. The right loan needs to fit alongside pensions, investments, tax planning and the rest of your life.

Overview

Borrowing that fits the bigger picture

For most people, a mortgage is the largest financial commitment they will make. The standard high-street comparison sites work fine when the situation is simple. They tend to fall over when it is not.

We work with clients whose situations are not standard. Director income with dividends and bonus. Multiple properties or buy-to-let portfolios. International assets or income. High-value purchases, bridging requirements, or borrowing that needs to fit alongside a wider financial plan.

Our advice is independent and our approach is consultative. We talk through the wider picture, look at what the borrowing is actually for, and find a lender who can handle it.

Where we tend to help

  • High-value residential mortgages
  • Buy-to-let and portfolio borrowing
  • Self-employed and director income
  • Bonus or dividend-heavy income
  • Bridging and short-term finance
  • International or offshore borrowing
  • Coordination with your wider plan
How we work

What to expect when you speak to us

Mortgage advice can feel transactional. We try to make ours less so, because the right answer depends on more than what the lender will offer.

01

The wider picture first

Before we look at lenders, we look at what the borrowing is for and how it fits with your pensions, savings, investments and tax position. The right mortgage is the one that supports the rest of the plan, not the one with the lowest headline rate.

02

Lenders for non-standard cases

We work with lenders who can handle director income, dividend-based pay, multiple income streams, and international assets. The big high-street names are not always the right starting point.

03

Independent advice

We are not tied to any single lender. We look across the market and recommend what fits your situation, including the rate, the terms, and the things buried in the small print.

04

Repayment, interest-only or a mix

The right structure depends on what the borrowing is for and how it sits alongside your other assets. We work through the trade-offs in plain English so you understand what you are choosing.

05

Fixing or floating

Whether to fix the rate, for how long, and what happens at the end of the period are decisions that come up every few years. We help you make them in the context of your wider plan, not in isolation.

06

Reviewing on schedule

Mortgages are not set-and-forget. We diary the end of fixed periods well in advance, so you have time to review options properly rather than rolling onto a standard variable rate by default.

The Financial Conduct Authority does not regulate Wills, Trusts or Tax advice. Tax treatment depends on individual circumstances and may be subject to change in the future. The value of your investments can go down as well as up, so you could get back less than you invested.
