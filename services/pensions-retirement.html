Pensions & Retirement Planning

Plan for a secure, flexible retirement with structured pension strategies and expert guidance, tailored to your circumstances and your goals.

Overview

A clearer view of your retirement

Retirement planning is about more than pensions alone. It's about understanding the lifestyle you want, the income you'll need to support it, and the right combination of pensions, investments and other assets to deliver it.

Our advisers take time to review your existing arrangements, identify gaps or inefficiencies, and bring everything together into a single, joined-up plan. Whether retirement is years away or just around the corner, we help you move forward with clarity and confidence.

How we help

  • Review and consolidation of existing pensions
  • Annual allowance and lifetime planning
  • Defined benefit transfer analysis
  • Income drawdown and annuity planning
  • Retirement cash flow modelling
  • Tax-efficient withdrawal strategies
Key considerations

What we'll explore together

01

Existing pensions

Where are they, what are they worth, and are they working as hard as they could be? We review charges, performance, and suitability.

02

Retirement income

How much will you need, when will you need it, and how can it be drawn most tax-efficiently across the years ahead?

03

Risk and reward

The right level of investment risk depends on your time horizon, your other resources and your attitude to volatility.

04

Tax efficiency

Pensions interact with allowances, inheritance tax and other rules in complex ways. Good planning makes a meaningful difference.

05

Family and legacy

How your pension is structured affects what passes to your family. We help you make those choices deliberately.

06

Ongoing review

Retirement plans need to flex with your circumstances and the rules around them. Regular reviews keep your plan relevant.

The Financial Conduct Authority does not regulate Wills, Trusts or Tax advice. The value of your investments can go down as well as up, so you could get back less than you invested. The guidance contained on this page is subject to the UK regulatory regime and is therefore primarily targeted at consumers based in the UK.
