Case Study

Helping a professional organise their lifetime financial plan

All client details have been anonymised. Names and identifying information have been changed to protect client confidentiality.

Adviser Daniel Cottam, Financial Planner
Area Personal financial planning, pension consolidation, protection, cash flow modelling

Situation

The client was a senior professional in her late forties working in the private sector. She was well-paid, owned her home jointly with her partner, and had been contributing to her workplace pension for many years. She also had three older pension pots from previous employers that she had never consolidated or reviewed.

She came to Aetas Wealth through a colleague recommendation. Her concern was not any particular financial problem but a general unease that her finances were fragmented: multiple pensions she had never tracked down properly, a protection policy taken out years ago that may no longer be appropriate, ISA contributions made inconsistently, and no clear sense of whether she was on track for the retirement she wanted.

Challenge

The main challenge was consolidation and clarity. Without a view of all her assets in one place, it was impossible to plan properly. The lost pension pots were a particular issue: one was with a scheme whose provider had changed twice, and the paperwork was incomplete. She also had no idea of the overall value, and the charges on the older pots were almost certainly materially higher than a modern arrangement.

Alongside this, her protection had not been reviewed since her circumstances had changed significantly — her income was higher, her mortgage had grown and she had a dependent child. There was a real gap between her existing cover and what would be needed.

Approach

Daniel Cottam began by tracing and consolidating the financial picture. With the client's authority, he traced the three older pension pots, obtained up-to-date valuations and reviewed the terms of each. Once the full picture was established, the options — consolidation into a single modern arrangement, transfer, or retention — were assessed against cost, risk and the client's objectives.

Cash flow modelling was built from this foundation: current assets, projected contributions, expected retirement date and income target in retirement. This model showed — for the first time — whether the client was on track and what adjustments would make a meaningful difference.

Protection was reviewed comprehensively. The existing policy was assessed against current income and liabilities, and a revised programme of life cover, critical illness and income protection was recommended and put in place — structured and costed within the overall financial plan rather than as a separate transaction.

An ISA strategy was also established as part of the plan: regular contributions structured to work alongside pension funding, with the ISA providing tax-free flexibility in the years approaching retirement.

Outcome

The client moved from fragmented financial arrangements to a clear, consolidated plan. The older pension pots were traced and, where appropriate, consolidated. Her protection was restructured to reflect her current circumstances. The cash flow model showed she was broadly on track for retirement at the age she had in mind, with clear levers — contribution levels, retirement date — that she could adjust as her circumstances evolved.

She described the process as making her feel genuinely in control of her finances for the first time in her adult life. The annual review process — part of the ongoing client relationship — means the plan stays current as her circumstances change.
